Why Metal Roofing Installation Is The Best Investment For Your Home


When protecting your home, the roof is one of the most essential aspects. It enhances your home's curb appeal and covers you and your loved ones from the elements. Therefore, investing in a roofing material that is durable, long-lasting, and requires minimal maintenance is crucial. Metal roofing is becoming increasingly popular as a reliable and efficient homeowner roofing solution. This blog post will explore why metal roofing installation is the best investment for your home. We will discuss the numerous benefits of metal roofing, its cost-effectiveness, the different types of metal roofing available, and the installation and maintenance processes. By the end of this post, you will better understand why metal roofing is a wise investment for your home and why it has become a popular choice for homeowners nationwide.

What Metal Roofing Is

Metal roofing is a system made from metal pieces or tiles specifically designed to protect homes and buildings from the elements. It is a durable, long-lasting roofing material designed to withstand harsh weather conditions such as heavy rain, strong winds, hail, and snow. Metal roofing is commonly made from steel, aluminum, copper, and zinc and can be manufactured in various styles, shapes, and colors to match any architectural design. The panels or tiles are interlocked and fastened to the roof decking using screws, clips, or other fastening systems to ensure a secure and watertight seal. Metal roofing is lightweight, making it easy to install and ideal for homes with lower structural support. With its durability and longevity, metal roofing is quickly becoming popular for homeowners looking for a roofing material that requires minimal maintenance and provides long-lasting protection.


Benefits Of Metal Roofing

Metal roofing offers a range of benefits, making it an excellent investment for homeowners. Here are some of the key benefits of metal roofing:

  • Durability and Longevity: Metal roofing is incredibly durable and can last up to 50 years or more, depending on the material and maintenance. It can withstand harsh weather conditions such as heavy rain, snow, hail, and high winds.

  • Energy Efficiency: Metal roofing is highly reflective and can reflect up to 70% of the sun's energy, reducing the heat absorbed into your home. This helps to lower your energy bills by reducing the need for air conditioning during hot weather.

  • Low Maintenance: Metal roofing requires minimal maintenance compared to other roofing materials like asphalt shingles or tiles. It is resistant to weather and debris and does not require regular cleaning or coating.

  • Increase in Home Value: Metal roofing can increase the value of your home due to its durability and energy efficiency. It is a long-term investment that can pay off when you sell your home.

  • Environmentally Friendly: Metal roofing is made from recycled materials and is 100% recyclable at the end of its life. It also reduces the energy needed for heating and cooling your home, making it a sustainable and eco-friendly roofing option.

Cost Of Metal Roofing

The cost of metal roofing can vary depending on several factors, including the type of metal used, the roof's style, the installation's complexity, and the home's location. Generally, metal roofing is more expensive upfront than traditional roofing materials like asphalt shingles. However, it is essential to consider the long-term savings and benefits of metal roofing.


The initial cost of metal roofing can range from $5 to $12 per square foot, which includes materials and labor. This can be higher than traditional roofing materials like asphalt shingles, which typically range from $3 to $5 per square foot. However, metal roofing is more durable and has a longer lifespan than asphalt shingles, which can require more frequent replacement.


The long-term savings of metal roofing make it a cost-effective option. Metal roofing is energy efficient and can lower your energy bills by reducing the need for air conditioning during hot weather. It also requires minimal maintenance, saving you money on repairs and replacements.


When determining the metal roofing cost, it is critical to talk with a competent roofing contractor to obtain an exact quote based on your specific requirements and location. They can guide the best type of metal roofing for your home and budget, the installation process, and any potential cost savings.

Types Of Metal Roofing

There are several types of metal roofing available for homeowners. Each type has its unique characteristics and advantages. Here are some of the most common types of metal roofing:

  • Steel Roofing: Steel roofing is the most popular type of metal roofing. It has several finishes, including galvanized, galvalume, and painted steel. It is durable, affordable, and can last up to 50 years or more.

  • Aluminum Roofing: Aluminum roofing is lightweight and corrosion-resistant, making it an excellent choice for coastal regions. It is also energy efficient and can reflect up to 95% of solar radiation, reducing energy costs.

  • Copper Roofing: Copper roofing is known for its distinctive appearance and is often used on high-end homes and buildings. It is also durable and can last over 100 years with proper maintenance.

  • Zinc Roofing: Zinc roofing is popular for modern homes and buildings. It is durable, long-lasting, and resistant to corrosion. It also develops a unique patina, giving it a distinctive appearance.

  • Standing Seam Roofing: Standing seam roofing is a type of metal roofing with interlocking panels raised above the roofline. It has a modern, sleek appearance is noted for its durability and leak resistance.

  • Metal Shingle Roofing: Metal shingle roofing is designed to mimic the appearance of traditional roofing materials like asphalt shingles or tiles. It comes in various colors and styles and is well-known for its durability and easy maintenance.

Installation Of Metal Roofing

The installation of metal roofing requires specialized knowledge and skills and should be done by a professional roofing contractor. Here are the general steps involved in the installation process:

  • Roof Inspection: The roofing contractor will inspect the existing roof to determine if it suits metal roofing. They will also take measurements and determine the amount of materials needed.

  • Preparation: The existing roof may need to be removed, or it may be possible to install metal roofing over the existing roof. The contractor will also prepare the roof by installing underlayment and ensuring proper ventilation.

  • Installation: The metal roofing panels will be installed in rows, starting at the bottom of the roof and working upwards. The panels will be attached to the roof deck using screws or clips, and the seams will be sealed to prevent leaks.

  • Flashing and Trim: Flashing and trim will be installed around the edges of the roof, as well as around chimneys, vents, and other openings.

  • Clean-Up: The contractor will clean up the job site and remove debris or materials.

The installation process can vary depending on the type of metal roofing and the job's complexity. Working with a professional roofing contractor with experience with metal roofing installation is essential to ensure that the job is done correctly and to avoid any potential problems with leaks or damage to the roof.


Proper installation is critical to the long-term durability and performance of metal roofing. When installed correctly, metal roofing can last 50 years or more, providing excellent value and protection for your home.


Maintenance Of Metal Roofing

Metal roofing requires minimal maintenance compared to other types of roofing materials. However, it is still essential to perform routine maintenance to ensure its longevity and performance. Here are some tips for maintaining your metal roof:

  • Keep it Clean: Regularly clean your metal roof to remove dirt, debris, and other buildup. This can be done with a hose or pressure washer, but use low pressure to avoid damaging the roof. Avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials, which can damage the roof's surface.

  • Inspect for Damage: Inspect your metal roof regularly and any other openings to ensure they are properly sealed and not allow water in. Address any issues promptly to prevent them from getting worse.

  • Trim Trees: Keep trees and branches trimmed away from your roof to prevent them from scratching the surface or causing damage in high winds.

  • Check Seals: Check the seals around vents, chimneys, and any other openings to ensure they are properly sealed and do not allow water.

  • Clear Gutters: Regularly clear your gutters and downspouts to prevent debris from accumulating and causing backups or water damage.

  • Professional Inspection: Have your metal roof inspected by a professional roofing contractor every few years to ensure its overall health and to address any potential issues.

In conclusion, metal roofing is an excellent long-term investment for your home. It offers numerous benefits, including durability, energy efficiency, and low maintenance requirements. With a lifespan of up to 50 years or more, metal roofing provides excellent value and protection for your home, making it a smart choice for homes looking for long-lasting roofing material.
